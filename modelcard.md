# Model Card## Model namePARP-BRCA Nascent Strand Gap Repair Proof-of-Concept Model## Model typeReduced mechanistic dynamical systems model with optional stochastic, compartmental, and synthetic fitting extensions.## Version summaryThis model extends a baseline deterministic ordinary differential equation framework to include:- parameter sweeps- local sensitivity analysis- stochastic lesion inflow- fork-proximal and fork-distal compartments- fitting to synthetic assay-like observables## SummaryThe model represents a conceptual framework for PARP inhibitor-induced replication-associated lesion formation and BRCA1-BRCA2-RAD51-dependent rescue. It is intended to capture qualitative system behavior, mechanistic dependencies, and analysis workflows rather than to provide experimentally calibrated quantitative predictions.The framework tracks unresolved lagging-strand intermediates, trapped PARP-DNA complexes, daughter-strand gap burden, RAD51-mediated repair intermediates, mature nascent DNA, secondary double-strand breaks, and survival.## Intended useAppropriate uses include:- conceptual exploration of PARP inhibitor response- mechanistic hypothesis generation- educational demonstration of reduced DNA repair modeling- exploration of perturbation effects through sweeps and sensitivity analysis- development of future model-fitting pipelines## Out-of-scope useThis model is not intended for:- clinical decision support- therapeutic selection in patients- quantitative viability prediction in real cell lines without calibration- diagnostic or regulatory use- replacement of experimental validation## Model inputsThe model accepts pathway and condition parameters including:- **?_LIG1**, relative canonical ligation activity- **?_BRCA1**, relative BRCA1 activity- **?_BRCA2**, relative BRCA2 activity- **I_PARPi**, normalized PARP inhibitor intensity- **?**, relative trapping potency- kinetic parameters such as **??**, **?**, **?**, and related coefficients- optional stochastic inflow parameters- optional compartment transfer parameters## Model outputsThe model returns trajectories and summaries for:- **L(t)**, ligatable discontinuities- **T(t)**, trapped PARP-DNA complexes- **G(t)**, daughter-strand gaps- **R(t)**, RAD51 repair intermediates- **M(t)**, matured nascent DNA- **D(t)**, secondary DSBs- **S(t)**, predicted survival- **?(t)**, repair vulnerability index- peak and AUC summary metrics- synthetic assay-like observables when requested## Extensions### Parameter sweepsUsed to examine graded transitions across control parameters such as PARP inhibitor intensity, BRCA2 activity, or LIG1 activity.### Local sensitivity analysisUsed to assess how perturbations in **??**, **?**, and **?** influence lesion burden or survival.### Stochastic lesion inflowIntroduces time-varying noise into lesion generation to explore heterogeneity.### Fork-proximal and fork-distal compartmentsSeparates near-fork lesion processing from more distal post-replicative gap states.### Synthetic assay fittingAllows **??**, **?**, and **?** to be fit against simulated assay-like data for comet assay, DNA combing, and RAD51 foci.## Core assumptionsThe model assumes that:1. unresolved lagging-strand intermediates are generated continuously during replication2. LIG1-dependent maturation reduces the unresolved intermediate pool3. PARP inhibitors increase trapped PARP-DNA complex burden4. trapped complexes and unresolved intermediates both contribute to daughter-strand gap formation5. BRCA1-BRCA2-RAD51 activity promotes rescue of daughter-strand gaps6. unresolved gaps contribute to secondary DSB formation and survival loss7. the reduced system is sufficient to capture major qualitative transitions of interest## LimitationsImportant limitations include:- no direct fit to a specific PubMed dataset- no explicit biochemical representation of all fork-associated intermediates- no explicit cell-cycle checkpoint or chromatin-state module- deterministic core behavior unless the stochastic extension is used- assay emulators are synthetic and not direct assay reconstructions- survival is a conceptual burden-based output rather than an experimentally calibrated viability model## Expected qualitative behaviorUnder the default proof-of-concept parameterization, the model is expected to show:- low lesion burden in untreated wild type- increasing gap and DSB burden with higher PARP inhibitor intensity- reduced RAD51 rescue in BRCA2-deficient states- increased upstream lesion pressure in LIG1-reduced states- strongest pathology in combined LIG1 and BRCA2-deficient PARP inhibitor-treated conditions## Evaluation guidanceThis model should be judged primarily by:- internal mechanistic coherence- biological plausibility of phenotype ordering- interpretability of perturbation effects- usefulness as a scaffold for future calibrationIt should not be judged by clinical predictive performance in its current form.## Risks and cautionsPotential misuse risks include:- interpreting synthetic observables as validated experimental data- assuming default parameter values are biologically fitted- treating survival outputs as directly translatable to experimental viability- overgeneralizing from a reduced model to all forms of replication stress biology## Maintenance and future developmentPotential future developments include:- calibration to real comet assay, DNA combing, or RAD51 foci datasets- probabilistic fitting and uncertainty quantification- explicit representation of fork reversal, repriming, and end-processing- richer stochastic and population-level simulations- benchmarking across inhibitor-specific trapping regimes
